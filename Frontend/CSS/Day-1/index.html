Weight Gain: Sample Diet Plan

This is a simple, general sample plan for someone underweight who wants to gain weight. Consult a doctor or registered dietitian for personalised advice.

Quick Notes

This sample plan aims for a moderate calorie surplus (for many people, about +300–600 kcal/day) and includes protein at each meal to support lean mass gain when combined with resistance training. If you have medical conditions, food allergies, or specific dietary needs, seek professional guidance.

Daily Sample Meal Plan

Aim to eat regularly, include calorie-dense foods, and prioritise protein.

Breakfast

Oats cooked with whole milk, 2 eggs (any style), 1 banana, and a handful of mixed nuts.

Estimated energy: ~600 kcal

Mid-morning snack

Greek yogurt (or full-fat curd) with honey and seeds; or a smoothie made with whole milk, fruit, and 1–2 tablespoons peanut butter.

Estimated energy: ~300 kcal

Lunch

Rice or 2–3 rotis, dal or lentils, a protein portion (paneer, chicken, or fish), cooked vegetables with a tablespoon of ghee or oil, and a serving of yogurt/raita.

Estimated energy: ~700 kcal

Afternoon snack

Peanut-chocolate shake, fruit & nut mix, or a sandwich with egg/cheese and a side of fruit.

Estimated energy: ~300 kcal

Dinner

Starchy base (potato, rice, or 2 rotis), protein (paneer, legumes, chicken, fish), vegetables cooked with oil, and a dairy side (glass of milk or curd).

Estimated energy: ~700 kcal

Before bed

Warm milk, a casein/protein shake, or peanut butter on toast.

Estimated energy: ~200 kcal

Practical Tips

  • Choose energy-dense foods: nuts, seeds, full-fat dairy, nut butters, avocados, dried fruit, and healthy oils to increase calories without huge volume.
  • Include protein at every meal — aim to meet protein needs (see protein targets below) especially if doing resistance training.
  • If appetite is low, drink smoothies/shakes that combine milk, fruit, nut butter, and a protein powder if available.
  • Do regular strength training (resistance exercises) to convert extra calories into muscle rather than only fat.
  • Weigh yourself weekly under consistent conditions and adjust calorie intake every 1–2 weeks if weight isn't increasing.

Protein Targets

A commonly used range for people doing resistance training is approximately 1.2–2.0 g of protein per kg of body weight per day. Adjust according to activity level and goals; if unsure, aim for the lower end and increase if you are training hard.

Grocery Ideas (mix & match)

Whole milk, paneer, eggs, chicken, fish, oats, rice, whole-wheat flour/rotis, peanut butter, almonds/walnuts, chia/flax seeds, avocados, potatoes, full-fat yogurt, olive/vegetable oil, dried fruits (raisins, dates), bananas, and other fruits you like.

Safety & When to Seek Help

If you are underweight due to illness, sudden weight loss, or poor appetite for a prolonged period, consult a healthcare professional. For personalised calorie targets, macronutrient breakdown, or meal planning (e.g., vegetarian/vegan adaptations), see a registered dietitian.

Note: This is a general sample plan for practice/education and is not personalised medical advice.
